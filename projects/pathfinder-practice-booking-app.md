# Pathfinder Practice Booking App

## Problem Solved

Practice booking and administrative workflows can become difficult to manage when appointments, notes, follow-ups, and availability are handled manually. This project provides a structured booking application to improve appointment visibility and support day-to-day practice operations.

## Business Context

The app is aimed at small practice operations that need a simple, maintainable booking workflow without unnecessary enterprise complexity. The emphasis is on clarity, reliability, and operational usefulness.

## Tech Stack

- Python
- Django and FastAPI-style backend patterns
- PostgreSQL
- Forms, validation, and structured records
- Authentication and administrative views

## Key Features

- Appointment and booking record management
- Availability-aware scheduling concepts
- Structured forms for capturing booking details
- Administrative views for tracking upcoming and historical appointments
- Validation to reduce incomplete or conflicting records
- Foundation for reporting and operational follow-up

## Data / Automation Work Involved

- Modeled booking and practice operations as structured relational data
- Automated validation around required fields, appointment status, and workflow state
- Created consistent records for review, reporting, and follow-up
- Reduced manual scheduling friction and fragmented tracking
- Designed the system to support future reminders, exports, or dashboards

## Testing and Quality Notes

- Tested booking creation, validation, status handling, and administrative flows
- Reviewed edge cases such as missing details, conflicting states, and invalid dates
- Excluded patient/client details, sensitive practice information, and deployment secrets
- Kept the portfolio case study focused on architecture and operational value
